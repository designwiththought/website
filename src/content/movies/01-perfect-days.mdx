A film about attention, stubbornly refusing to be a film about work. Re-watch; better the second time. I cried at a tree.

Wenders has been making films about looking for fifty years, and this one is the quiet distillation. The protagonist's job is incidental; the camera's job is the whole text. I went in expecting a film about routine and came out with a film about reverence, which I think is the same film if you sit with it long enough.
