## Premise

Most teams I've worked with treat "screen readers" as a single thing. They are not. VoiceOver, NVDA, JAWS, TalkBack, Narrator, Orca, and ChromeVox all implement the same specification slightly differently — and the differences are precisely the places your product will break for someone.

The field guide is a thirty-two page booklet that lays the differences out, side by side, with drawings. It is the document I wish a senior had handed me in my first week of accessibility work.

## What's in it

- A short history of each screen reader (one page each), so you know who makes it and for whom.
- A comparison of how they announce the same ARIA patterns — dialogs, tabs, live regions, menus.
- A "here's what typically breaks" section for each, with the specific HTML or ARIA construct at fault.
- A glossary of the terminology each uses, because the names are not the same (is it a "heading list," an "outline," or a "rotor"?).
