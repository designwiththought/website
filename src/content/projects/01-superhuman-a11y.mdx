When I joined Superhuman, accessibility wasn't ignored so much as undefined. There was no audit process, no shared language for what "accessible" meant in the context of a keyboard-first email client, and no clear owner. My first job was to make the invisible visible: catalog every interaction, measure it against WCAG 2.2, and build a prioritized backlog that engineering could actually work against.<Footnote id="1" text="WCAG 2.2 became a W3C Recommendation in October 2023." />

The hardest part wasn't the technical work. It was building consensus that accessibility deserved sprint time alongside feature work. That meant showing up in planning meetings with data, not just principles.<Footnote id="2" text="Marcy Sutton's work on accessibility testing automation was a key influence on our audit tooling." />
