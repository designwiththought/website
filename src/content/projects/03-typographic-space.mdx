This started as a weekend experiment and became a recurring creative outlet. The premise is simple: what if you treated a letterform not as a symbol but as a floor plan? Each glyph becomes a room, a corridor, a structural element. The compositions are generated programmatically but curated by hand.<Footnote id="1" text="Inspired by Zuzana Licko's work on the relationship between type design and architectural thinking." />

There's no practical application here. That's the point. Not everything needs to ship. Sometimes the most useful thing you can do as a designer is make something that exists only because you were curious.<Footnote id="2" text="Frank Chimero's The Shape of Design makes this case more eloquently than I can." />
