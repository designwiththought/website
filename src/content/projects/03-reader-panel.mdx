## Premise

Product copy is usually reviewed by the people who wrote it. That is not a review; it is a ratification. The reader panel is a standing group of twenty-four strangers who read our drafts before we ship them — and tell us, gently, what we actually said.

Half the panel uses assistive technology (screen readers, magnification, voice control). The other half doesn't. Every draft of significance gets at least two reads from each half.

## How it runs

- A shared doc goes out on Thursday with a short brief: what the copy is for, what we're worried about, the two questions we want answered.
- Panel members leave comments by Monday — inline, specific, dated.
- We synthesise on Tuesday, rewrite on Wednesday, and send a "here's what changed" note to the panel on Friday.

> The comments from the screen-reader half are, almost without exception, more useful than the comments from our designers. I used to find that unsettling; now I find it clarifying.

## What we pay for it

Panel members are paid per review, in line with an entry-level designer's hourly rate. The rate is non-negotiable. We do not ask for free labour; we do not pay in "exposure." Budget for this lives with the writing team, not with research.

## What I'd like to change

The panel is too American, too native-English. I have been trying, slowly, to add four members who work in English as a second language. If you know someone, write to me.
