## Premise

Most keyboard shortcut designs are inherited, undocumented, and slightly wrong. People copy the shortcuts they know from other products, forget to test them with a screen reader, and ship — then a ticket arrives six months later from a user who cannot close a dialog without a mouse.

Quiet Keys is a library I started maintaining as a centralised reference for the patterns that actually work, and the ones that quietly don't.

## What's in it

Forty-odd interaction patterns. Each entry has:

- The keybinding(s), and the combinations to avoid
- When to use it — and when not
- The screen-reader behaviour it produces on VoiceOver, NVDA, and JAWS
- Its *escape hatch*: the way out if you're caught in it
- Notes from production use, with receipts

## The goal

Stop designing keyboard shortcuts alone. Borrow from the library. Contribute back when you find something the library does not cover, or quietly gets wrong.

## Where it's going

The next release folds in a small test harness — a set of scripts you can run against any component to confirm it implements the pattern correctly under a screen reader. Teams at three companies are using it. I'd like to hear from more.
