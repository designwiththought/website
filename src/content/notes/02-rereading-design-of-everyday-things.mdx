Rereading *The Design of Everyday Things*. Norman is still right about almost everything, and still wrong about the same few things. I think the wrong bits are the most useful.

The right bits give you vocabulary; the wrong bits tell you where the field has moved since the book was written. Both are worth the ride, but only one of them teaches you to read with a pencil in your hand.
