The cheapest good book in the world. Give copies to juniors.

Berger's introduction is still, fifty years on, the clearest short writing I know on what it means to look. I keep a small stack of secondhand copies in the office bookshelf and hand them out at the beginning of every quarter, which is the tradition I am most proud of as a manager.
