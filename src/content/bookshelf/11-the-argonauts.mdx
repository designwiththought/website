The single most-marked-up book I own.

I bought this in 2016 with a clean intention to read it cover to cover, and ten years later I am still inside it. The form is the argument: a memoir that is also a treatise that is also a love letter, sliding between modes without letting any one of them dominate. Most of my notebooks of the last decade have a Nelson sentence on the first page.
