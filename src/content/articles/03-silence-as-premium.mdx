The first six months of managing a design team, I kept opening Figma files and moving things around. Not because my team asked me to, but because I missed the work. It took a while to realize that every pixel I pushed was a signal to my team that I didn't trust their judgment.<Footnote id="1" text="Julie Zhuo's The Making of a Manager was the first book that made this tension feel normal." />

Good management is mostly about asking better questions. Instead of solving the problem yourself, you learn to ask what they've tried, what's blocking them, and whether they need a decision or just a sounding board. The answer is usually the last one.<Footnote id="2" text="This framework comes from Michael Lopp's writing on the 1:1 as the most important meeting you'll have." />
