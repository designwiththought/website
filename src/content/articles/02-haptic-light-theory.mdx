Engineers don't ship inaccessible products because they don't care. They ship them because nobody in the room asked the question. Accessibility work gets deprioritized the same way documentation does. Everyone agrees it matters, and then it loses every sprint negotiation.<Footnote id="1" text="WebAIM's annual survey consistently finds that the most common accessibility barriers are basic HTML issues, not complex ARIA problems." />

The fix isn't training. Training helps, but what actually moves the needle is making accessibility a blocking criterion in your definition of done, and having a manager who will hold that line when the deadline gets tight.<Footnote id="2" text="Lainey Feingold's Structured Negotiation outlines how organizational accountability drives better outcomes than lawsuits." />
