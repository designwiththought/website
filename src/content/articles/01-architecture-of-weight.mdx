Most of what makes a design system work isn't the components. It's the decisions baked into spacing scales, color tokens, and type hierarchies. things nobody on the team actively thinks about after the first week.<Footnote id="1" text="Nathan Curtis has written extensively about this in his Design Systems Handbook." />

A 4px grid isn't interesting. But the moment someone on the team has to decide between 12px and 16px of padding, and there's no clear answer, the system has already failed them. The job of a design system isn't to look good in a Figma library. It's to make the next decision obvious.<Footnote id="2" text="This framing borrows from Ethan Marcotte's work on design system governance." />
