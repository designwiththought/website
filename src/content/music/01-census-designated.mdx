Lives on in the car. The kind of record where production choices keep rearranging themselves on the 20th listen. I keep finding small rooms inside it.

Some records announce their structure on the first play and then spend the rest of your life proving it. This one hides the structure on purpose — a hi-hat moves three feet to the left between listens, a chorus that you swore was loud arrives quiet, and you start to suspect the album is paying attention to you back.
