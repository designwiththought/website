I’m Brian. I lead accessibility at Superhuman, which some of you will still remember as Grammarly. Before that, I was a designer at Figma, an engineer at The Guardian, and — for a short, useful stretch — a two-person consultancy with a friend.

I’ve spent about equal time as a designer, a developer, and a manager of teams that include both. The longer I do this, the less those three labels feel like separate jobs — they feel like three rooms in the same house, with doors between them that I keep propped open.

Most of what I care about, professionally, comes back to one word: care. Care for the people using the software. Care for the engineers who have to maintain it. Care for the systems — the words, the components, the processes — that hold a product together when the humans are busy elsewhere.

This site is where I think in public. Essays are the long stuff; studies are the investigated stuff; notes are the unfinished stuff. Projects are what I’m actually building. The now page is the short version.
