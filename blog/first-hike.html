Silver Falls Loop — First Hike of Spring

March 15, 2026 · 4 min read

8.5 miles through waterfalls and old-growth forest at Silver Falls State Park. A perfect way to shake off winter.

Trail
Trail of Ten Falls (full loop)
Distance
8.5 miles
Elevation
~800 ft gain
Conditions
Overcast, 48°F, muddy in spots

The Hike

I started at the South Falls Lodge around 8am, early enough to have most of the trail to myself. The parking lot was nearly empty — one of the perks of going on a Saturday morning in March when everyone else is still waiting for summer.

South Falls is the star of the park. You walk behind a 177-foot waterfall. Behind it. The mist hits your face and for a second you forget you're less than two hours from Portland.1

Trail Conditions

Muddy in the lower sections, especially between Lower South Falls and Drake Falls. Nothing impassable, but I was glad to have waterproof boots.

The old-growth sections between Double Falls and North Falls are where this trail really shines. Massive Douglas firs, thick moss on everything, that particular Pacific Northwest silence that isn't really silence at all.2

Highlights

  • South Falls — the classic. Walking behind it never gets old.
  • North Falls — possibly even more impressive, far fewer people
  • Double Falls to Drake Falls — quiet, mossy, otherworldly

Total time on trail: about 4 hours. 42.3 miles logged for the year — on pace for my 200-mile goal.

Footnotes

  1. Silver Falls State Park is the largest state park in Oregon at over 9,000 acres. ↑
  2. Gordon Hempton has called the Pacific Northwest one of the quietest places in the continental US. ↑
